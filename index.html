Ambient

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

This term has other meanings, see Ambient.

Ambient (eng. Ambient "surrounding") is a style of electronic music based on the modulations of the sound timbre. Ambient is often characterized by atmospheric, enveloping, unobtrusive, background sound. Born in the 1970s, thanks to the work of Brian Eno; has since formed several varieties of. Gained popularity in the early 1990s thanks to artists such as The Orb and Aphex Twin.

Ambient distribution

Nevertheless, ambient remains an extremely vague concept, since this term can refer to performers of various other genres, for example, performers of "academic" experimental electronics (Chris Meloche, David Shea, Asmus Tietchens, David Toop), space music (Robert Rich, Steve Roach, Vidna Obmana, Klaus Schulze, Jeff Greinke, Alio Die), dub (Bill Laswell, The Orb, Mick Harris, Drome, Woob), ethno electronics (Jon Hassell, Banco de Gaia, Astralasia), industrial (Lustmord, Merzbow, Final, Aube, James Plotkin), club culture hybrids (The KLF, Global Communication, Mixmaster Morris, Biosphere, Higher Intelligence Agency, Sun Electric, Tetsu Inoue, David Moufang, LTJ Bukem, Autechre).

In 2002, music producer Jeff Tone and journalist Peter Manzi of New Age Voice Magazine ranked the top ambient albums of all time. Experts were invited to select applicants, among whom were musicians Steve Roach , Robert Rich, the founder of the record company Hearts of Space Stephen Hill and others. As a result, the experts chose "25 major ambient albums of all time". This ranking includes both the classic works of Brian Eno and Steve Roach, as well as albums commonly classified as progressive rock. ( Tangerine Dream ), electronic music (Constantin Grand , Jean Ven Robert Hal , Aphex Twin , The Orb) and even jazz (Miles Davis). Thus, the very definition of the concept of "ambient" was significantly expanded.

Ambient is often heard in film and video game soundtracks, especially fantasy ones . Notable films with ambient sound design: Forbidden Planet (1956), A Space Odyssey 2001 (1968), A Clockwork Orange (1971), Solaris (1972), Eraserhead (1977), Stalker (1979), The Shining (1980), Blade Runner (1982), Abyss (1987; based on Ray Bradbury's Chicago Abyss), Dead Calm (1989), Safety (1995), Lost Highway (1997), Traffic (2000), Mulholland Drive (2001), Donnie Darko (2001), 28 Days Later (2002), Solaris (2002), Collision (2004), Silent Hill (2006), The Prestige (2006), Mister Loneliness (2007), Inferno (2007), The Lovely Bones (2009), The Social Network (2010), Drive (2011), Oblivion (2013), Gravity (2013), Gone Girl (2014).

Musical characteristic

Brian Eno's albums Ambient 1 - Music for Airport (1978) and Ambient 4 - On Land (1982) served as milestones in the development of ambient as a style. Ambient 1 - Music for Airport contains soft, light intonations and is close to academic music: acoustic piano, female three-parts formed the basis of the album. Ambient 4 - On Land - a darker and more experimental canvas, in addition to live musicians, ethnic and noise percussion, field and concrete recordings were used here.

History

Background

One of the early forerunners of ambient was the furniture music of the innovative French composer Eric Satie. He invented it in 1914-1916, and according to his own idea, furniture music should serve as a background for everyday life, shopping in a store, receiving guests, eating in a restaurant, and all other daily activities. The basic principle of constructing new music was the arbitrary repetition of one or more sound cells (or a thematic phrase) an unlimited number of times.

Furnishing music became the basis for minimalism, another immediate forerunner of ambient. Half a century after Satie, American composer John Milton Cage discovers furnishing music and creates his own minimalist works based on it. A few years later, Steve Reich, John Adams, Philip Glass and many others will follow his path.

In 1968, Holger Shukai recorded the Canaxis album, overlaying electronic hum on an accidentally discovered tape of Vietnamese folk songs. According to Gorokhov, this was ambient. A lot of different music of the early 1970s reached almost ambient. For example, Bob Norten and other jazz artists, or the Gravity Adjusters Expansion Band. Pioneers of electronic music Mike Oldfield, Jean-Michel Jarre, Klaus Schulze, Vangelis, Eduard Artemyev created ambient compositions, as well as progressive rock figures - Pink Floyd, Yes. Kraut rock bands Tangerine Dream, Popol Vuh, Can, Kraftwerk made major contributions.

Ambient appearance

The term "ambient" appeared in the 70s of the XX century, to refer to music that, according to Brian Eno , can be "actively listened to with attention, or simply ignored, depending on the choice of the listener." Brian Eno is credited with coining the term into music. According to the prevailing legend, the idea of creating music, today called ambient, came to Ino's head when he was lying at home with a fracture and, for lack of other things to do, listened to the noises and sounds coming from the street. In them, he caught a certain regularity and musicality.

Brian Eno (former keyboardist for Roxy Music) moved from rock music to the field of experimental music and in 1975 discovered the technical side of the process of obtaining an enveloping sound, which consists in getting the original signal through a graphic equalizer into two tape recorders connected in parallel, in which the sound is reproduced with a delay at a certain interval, then from one tape recorder, additionally processed, it is output to the speaker system, and from another tape recorder it returns to the initial conversion point. According to Eno himself, the sound transformed through his "ambient system" is perceived as "part of the vibrations of the environment, like the color of a calm diffused light or the rustle of rain." Eno also formulated the second important principle of ambient - the development of music at its own expense, giving the performer the role of a passive creator.

Eno acknowledged the influence of Eric Satie, John Cage, as well as the influence of the drone music of La Monte Young (of which he said "La Monte Young is our dad") and the mood music of Miles Davis, especially his epic piece "He Loved Him Madly" ("He loved him madly") from the 1974 album Get Up with It, of which Eno wrote that "the piece felt 'spacious', a quality I often returned to."

Knock Bambuk at 11 o'clock is a Soviet musical group formed in the late 1980s. In their music, they anticipated such styles as trip-hop and downtempo. After recording the album "Easy business cold" in 1991, the band ceased to exist.

Development in the 1990s

Since the early 1990s, a whole wave of new artists have emerged who continue to develop ambient in various sub-genres and derivative genres. Ambient sound is now emphasized by rhythm or melody. Derived genres ambient house, ambient techno, IDM, downbeat, downtempo, ambient dub, ambient breakbeat, illbient are rhythmic ambient, and in most cases instrumental (exceptions: downbeat and downtempo).

  • The term ambient house indiscriminately referred to 1990s artists (The Orb, The KLF, Irresistible Force, Future Sound of London, Orbital), both performing dance music and not necessarily. In a stricter sense, ambient house combines certain elements of ambient and acid house. Subsequently, it was replaced by more specific terms and is rarely used.
  • A specific reorientation of the term ambient house is ambient techno, which is usually applied to B12, early Aphex Twin, The Black Dog, Higher Intelligence Agency, Biosphere and others. These artists are distinguished by their closeness to the techno and electro genres and are most often released on the labels Apollo, GPR, Warp and others. Over time, the term ambient techno evolved into IDM without any stylistic changes in the music.
  • IDM was the reaction of dance music producers to accusations of the latter's stupid monotony. There have been attempts to name the genre differently: "electronic listening music" (Warp Records version) or "braindance" (Aphex Twin version). Artists like Mixmaster Morris and Dr. Alex Paterson mixed Chicago house, softer synthpop and ambient, creating a new wave inspired by a variety of sources. Warp Records' Artificial Intelligence compilation introduced half a dozen of the style's most important exponents to listeners around the world: Aphex Twin, The Orb, Plastikman, Autechre, The Black Dog and B12.
  • Given the amount and variety of ambient music in the 1990s, the term downbeat is sometimes used to replace such concepts as ambient house and ambient techno, however, in its broadest definition, downbeat is electronic music for the house, not for the dance floor. Representatives: Morcheeba, Thievery Corporation, Jazzanova, Zero 7, Howie B.
  • Similar to downbeat is the downtempo genre, where the performers are more beat-oriented than the ambient component, but not as much as in trip-hop. Representatives: Gang Gang Dance, Rhye, Dobie.
  • Since the English label Beyond Records released a series of albums called "Ambient Dub" in the early 1990s, the term ambient dub has been used to refer to rhythmic ambient performers incorporating elements of Jamaican dub (e.g. reverb, emphasis on bass and drums, heavy use effects). Although the term ambient dub has fallen out of fashion, it remains useful for referring to a denser sub-type of downtempo. Representatives include The Orb, Techno Animal, Automaton, Solar Quest.
  • There is also an ambient breakbeat with a strong hip hop influence, but less energetic than trip hop. Prominent examples are DJ Wally and Req.
  • Illbient is based on ambient, but can include dub, hip-hop, drum and bass, as well as any ethnic music, it can be creepy and a little dark, or unpredictable and dressed in noise, but it always retains its leisurely pace. Representatives of illbient includeDJ Spooky, We, Byzar, Tipsy, Specter, Rob Swift, Badawi.
  • Combining ambient with psychedelic trance is called saibient.
  • The chillout trend has also gained popularity.

Subgenres

Dark ambient

Main article: Dark ambient

"Industrial" culture discovered ambient through a loop (loop) - a looped piece of magnetic tape with a fragment of a recording, after a long listening to which an "internal wave effect" occurs, when a new fragment is born or imagined in a repeatedly repeated sound pattern, which gradually becomes brighter and brighter and comes to the fore, changing the main melody of the work. The second important invention was the drone (drone) - a multi-channel low-frequency rumble that makes up the mystical landscape of the work.

Brian Williams (better known as Lustmord ) began releasing albums in 1980 that changed the foundations of ambient. Previously perceived as a relaxing experience and a background layer of everyday convenience, ambient suddenly acquired an almost religious aspect and functions of emotional theater. Lustmord became a pioneer of dark ambient â€” a symbiosis of low-frequency pulsations, fragments of ceremonial music and singing, recordings of natural noises, and a long rhythmic beat.

Lowercase

Main article: Lowercase

Lowercase is an extreme form of minimalist ambient where very quiet sounds are framed by long silences.

Space ambient

Main article: Space ambient

Space ambient or space music is a genre that is characterized by a measured, slow (rarely medium) tempo of sound. Synthetic, deaf and monotonous sounds are used, which, as a rule, are looped and serve as a background, on which additional rhythmic electronic losses are superimposed as the composition progresses. Often, muffled choral singing is used, disguised as music and modulations of synthetic melodies. It has a strong immersive atmosphere, calming and relaxing and meditative effects on the listener. Often found in soundtracks for space-themed games. Notable Artists: Michael Stearns, Jonn Serrie, Constance Demby, Telomere, Between Interval, UOK, Sula Bassana.

Ambient dub

Ambient dub is a style that embodies the mixing of ambient with dub . The term was first used by the now-defunct Birmingham label Beyond Records in the early 1990s. The label released a series of albums, Ambient Dub Volume 1-4, which inspired many artists to release music in the genre, including Bill Laswell, who used a similar name for a series of his albums under the pseudonym Divination where he collaborated with other artists in the genre. Ambient dub correlates with dub styles popularized by King Tubbyand other Jamaican sound artists of the 1960s and early 1970s using ambient electronics that were inspired by early types of Jamaican dijeng. The style is characterized by drop-outs, echo, equalization and various psychedelic electronic effects. The genre often uses overdubbing techniques and contains elements of ethnic music, deep basses and harmonic sounds. In the words of writer and performer David Toop, "Dub music is like a long delay of echoes repeating in time... that turns the rational order of musical sequences into an ocean of feelings". Notable artists in the genre: Dreadzone, Higher Intelligence Agency, The Orb, Gaudi.

Development and related genres

Psybient

Main article: Psybient

Psybient is a genre of electronic music that combines elements of psychedelic trance, ambient, downtempo, dub, ethnic music, and new age music.

Influence on other genres

Ambient has been a major influence on New Age music. The roots of pre-ambient instrumental new age (Kitaro, Michael Hedges, Susan Chani, George Winston, Andreas Vollenweider) lie in jazz, folk, classical and synthesizer music. From the second half of the 1980s, new age, under the influence of ambient, turns into a song genre with a strong electronic component (Eniya , Enigma). Ambient also influenced the development of gothic rock, which resulted in ethereal wave.

In the 1990s, sub-genres of alternative rock, such as post-rock or dream pop, are experiencing ambient influences. In metal, "atmospheric" sounds are becoming popular.
